Wisconsin Land Patent — 1848

Investigation of a federal land patent issued to Pervis Andrews in Dodge County, Wisconsin. March 28, 2026.

The Patent

Confirmed — BLM / General Land Office

Pervis Andrews and Edward West (joint patentees) received 142.58 acres in Dodge County, Wisconsin, on May 10, 1848. Cash purchase through the Green Bay Land Office at $1.25/acre (~$178 total, split between two buyers). Located in Township 11N, Range 16E, Section 18 — present-day Burnett or Westford Township, south-central Dodge County. The parcels were "government lots" along a water feature.

What Was This About?

Perviz didn't move to Wisconsin. He was a clothier in Salem, New York, in 1850 — two years after this purchase. Neither he nor his partner Edward West appears in any Wisconsin census. This was almost certainly speculative land investment, not homesteading.

The late 1840s were the peak of Wisconsin's first land boom. After the Black Hawk War cleared Native title and federal surveys opened millions of acres, land agents traveled to eastern cities — setting up in taverns, courthouses, and churches to sell Wisconsin land by the parcel. They'd show plat maps, describe the water features and mill sites, and take cash on the spot. Groups from the same community would buy lots in the same section on the same day.

That's exactly what the record shows. Three patents were issued on May 10, 1848 for land in the same section: Perviz Andrews and Edward West, plus James R. Whaley. A land agent almost certainly came through the Washington County, New York area in early 1848, and these men bought in together. Perviz and West split the cost of a premium waterfront parcel. The plan was probably to flip the land or relocate later. When neither happened, the land was likely sold or abandoned. Perviz moved to Indiana instead, around 1853–1855.

Other Andrews in Dodge County

A Lucas Andrews (also spelled "Lucus") patented land in the adjacent township (12N-15E) in 1846 and 1848. The proximity was noted but explored — no connection to Perviz was established. The name does not appear in Washington County, NY or Marshall County, IN records alongside the known Andrews families.

Other Names to Trace

  • Edward West — Perviz's joint patentee. A business partner, neighbor, or possibly relative by marriage. Search Washington County, NY census and land records for an Edward West connected to the Andrews or O'Dell families.
  • James R. Whaley — filed on the same date in the same section. Likely part of the same batch purchase. May have been from the same New York community.

What Happened to the Land

The Dodge County Register of Deeds and the BLM Tract Book (Volume 958) would show whether Perviz sold the land, when, and to whom. If the land was still in his name when he died in 1856, it would have passed through his estate — and any probate record in either New York or Indiana could reference it.
