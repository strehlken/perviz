Key Revelations

Perviz was alone in 1840. The Chautauqua County census shows him with no wife or children — he married Julia after 1840, probably around 1843–1846. A Wisconsin land patent (May 1848, Dodge County) adds unexpected geography. And the 1860 Fulmer household census shows the complete blended family, including a son named Perviz Fulmer, born ~1855.

Wisconsin Land Patent — 1848

Confirmed — US General Land Office Records

Pervis Andrews received a federal land patent in Dodge County, Wisconsin, issued May 10, 1848, through the Green Bay Land Office. Township 11-N, Range 16-E, Section 18. Accession: WI1210__.227, Document 4218.

This is two years before the 1850 census places him in Salem, New York. He apparently acquired Wisconsin land but never relocated there — this was either a speculative investment or a failed homestead attempt. The land's subsequent disposition (sale, forfeiture, transfer) could be traced through Dodge County deed records.

Timing Problem — Perviz Fulmer

The 1860 census lists Perviz Fulmer as age 5, placing his birth at ~1855. But Julia didn't marry Jonas Fulmer until July 10, 1856 — after Perviz Andrews died in April 1856. If the boy was truly born in 1855, he was born before Julia married Jonas, and while Perviz Andrews was still alive. Possible explanations: (1) the age is slightly off and he was born in late 1856 or early 1857, after the marriage; (2) Julia and Jonas's relationship began before Perviz's death; (3) the child is actually Perviz Andrews's biological son, adopted by Jonas. The name "Perviz" for the child makes most sense as a memorial to the deceased first husband.
