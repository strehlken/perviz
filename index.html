The tombstone in Marshall County, Indiana, reads Perviz. Census takers wrote it as Parvis, Pervis, or simply P. It is a Persian name meaning "victorious" — wildly unusual for a Vermont-born tradesman in the early nineteenth century. The name's rarity is both the central frustration and the greatest asset in this research: there are almost no other Perviz Andrews entries in the historical record, but when one appears, it can only be him.

Perviz Andrews was born in 1802 in Bennington, Vermont. He was a tailor. He married a woman named Julia Odell — or possibly Julia C. something-else — from Troy, New York. They had two children in the 1840s: George W. Andrews and Cynthia Ann Andrews. The family moved to a small town in Indiana. Perviz died before July 1856, possibly of typhoid. Julia then married a local widower, and they named their first son Perviz, after her late husband.

That was the sum total of what was known. Nothing about Perviz's parents, his siblings, or his early life. Nothing about Julia's maiden name or parents. Nothing connecting this family to anyone who came before them.

Two questions drive this investigation: Who were the parents of Perviz Andrews? And who was Julia Odell before she married him?

This document records the findings of a single evening's intensive research — combining primary source documents, census records, marriage certificates, obituaries, church records, and county histories — that cracked several of these dead ends open and identified clear paths for further work.

The Starting Facts

Before this research session, the following facts were established through family knowledge and prior investigation:

Perviz Andrews

  • Born 1802 in Bennington, Vermont
  • Occupation: tailor
  • Tombstone spells his name Perviz; census records vary (Parvis, Pervis)
  • Married Julia Odell (or O'Dell), born approximately 1818 in Troy, New York
  • The marriage likely occurred in the early 1840s
  • Children: George W. Andrews (b. ~1846) and Cynthia Ann Andrews (b. ~1844)
  • The family moved to Indiana — specific location unknown
  • Perviz died approximately 1856, possibly of typhoid or similar illness
  • Julia's middle initial was C; family speculation was that C might represent her true maiden name, with Odell being a prior married name

After Perviz's Death

  • Julia married a local widower who already had children
  • They named their first son together Perviz, after her late husband
  • The family moved to Kansas
  • George W. Andrews, as a grown man, also went to Kansas
  • George married Hortense White, whose family came from Michigan
  • Cynthia E. Andrews married Orrin White, Hortense's brother. They lived in Northern California. Nellie visited Aunt Cynthia there by train from San Francisco.
  • Their daughter was Nelly Andrews

Prior Research Attempts

  • Correspondence with Odell researchers, including a contact in Virginia — no connections found
  • No records found for the Andrews family in the Bennington, Vermont, or Troy, New York, areas
  • The unusual name "Perviz" had not been located in any other period documents

What We Established

The Location: Marshall County, Indiana

Confirmed — Marriage Record

Julia C. Andrews married Jonas Fulmer on July 10, 1856, in Marshall County, Indiana. Recorded in Marriage Book A, page 105. Marriage Cert

This establishes that Perviz died before this date, and that the family was in Marshall County. Julia used her Andrews married name with the middle initial C.

Confirmed — Obituary

Jonas Fulmer was born May 16, 1812, in Herkimer County, New York. He married first wife Sarah Ann Vanhorn on February 9, 1834. He moved to St. Joseph County, Indiana, in 1838, then to Marshall County ~1839. Sarah Ann died there. He then married Julia (Andrews). Jonas died March 1, 1880. Buried North Liberty Cemetery. Survived by sons Marion and Parvis Fulmer, plus two sisters and four children total. Concordia Republican, March 25, 1880

Confirmed — Marriage Record

George W. Andrews, age 25, married Hortense White, age 21, on December 4, 1871, in Manhattan, Kansas. Recorded in Cloud County marriage records. This places George's birth at ~1846. Cloud County KS

Corrected — Newspaper Evidence Overrides User Tree

Cynthia E. Andrews (not "Cynthia Ann" as previously reported) was born approximately 1844–1845 in Indiana. She was Perviz's biological daughter. The Ancestry user tree claiming she married Ezra Hayes and died in Yamhill, Oregon, in 1914 is incorrect — newspaper clippings show her alive in Indiana in 1920.

Newspaper evidence documents her as Mrs. Cynthia E. White marrying Henry A. Adle of Walkerton, St. Joseph County, Indiana (undated clipping). A second clipping from the South Bend Tribune, November 9, 1920 reports Mrs. Cynthia E. Adle of Walkerton marrying William H. LaFong of Noble County, Indiana. That article states this was "the fourth marriage for the woman. All the previous unions were ended by death."

Her four husbands in sequence: (1) Orrin White (brother of Hortense White; lived in Northern California), (2) unknown, (3) Henry A. Adle, (4) William H. LaFong. She remained in the Walkerton / St. Joseph County area throughout her life. Her death record — likely in St. Joseph County or nearby, sometime after 1920 — could name her parents and resolve the question of Perviz's parentage.

The White connection is not a coincidence: Cynthia married Orrin White, brother of Hortense White, who married Cynthia's brother George W. Andrews. This was a double sibling marriage. Cynthia and Orrin lived in Northern California (Wheatland, Yuba City, or Red Bluff area). George and Hortense's daughter Nellie, living in San Francisco, visited Aunt Cynthia there by train. After Orrin's death, Cynthia returned to Indiana.

Daniel Andrews is eliminated as Perviz's relative. He was born January 10, 1810, in Cayuga, New York. His father was Robert Andrews (b. ~1793) and mother Persa (b. ~1793). Robert was only 9 years old when Perviz was born in 1802 — Daniel is from a separate Andrews family. FamilySearch

Lyman H. Andrews — Key Records

Confirmed — Death Certificate of Son Charles R. Andrews

Charles R. Andrews (March 25, 1838 – July 4, 1913), born Wyoming County, New York. The death certificate names his father as Lyman H. Andrews, birthplace Vermont, and his mother as Mary Jane Yeakley, birthplace New York. Informant was Frank M. Andrews. IN Death Cert

Confirmed — Old Settlers Society, 1878

Lyman H. Andrews signed the Old Settlers Society roster in 1878, recording his year of settlement in Marshall County as 1839 and his place of birth as Rensselaer County, New York. McDonald 1881

Confirmed — Road Hands Record

Lyman H. Andrews served as road supervisor, District 10, Center Township, Marshall County in 1842 and 1844. His road crew included Austin Fuller (who later officiated Andrews family marriages and served as county auditor) and William S. Yeckley (almost certainly related to his wife Mary Jane Yeakley). County Treasurer Records

Confirmed — County Officers List

Lyman H. Andrews served as Coroner of Marshall County (date imprecise — records poorly kept for early coroners). McDonald 1890

The Camden Valley Baptism

Confirmed — Church Record

On July 30, 1843, at the Camden Valley Church of the United Brethren in Washington County, New York, a group of adults were baptized by minister E. Rondthaler Jr. Among those baptized: Perviz Andrews. Church Record

Washington County, NY, lies directly between Bennington, VT (Perviz's birthplace) and Troy, NY (Julia's reported birthplace). The Camden Valley area (White Creek / Cambridge) is approximately 20 miles from each. This places Perviz in exactly the right geography to have met and married Julia Odell in the early 1840s.

Other adults baptized the same day included members of the Harris, Gray, Bristol, and Smith families. Notably, Stephen Andrews married Lucretia Harris in Marshall County, Indiana, in 1863 — suggesting the Harris family may have migrated alongside the Andrews family from Washington County to Indiana.

What Remains Unproven

Unproven — The Perviz–Lyman Relationship

Both Perviz Andrews (b. 1802, Bennington, VT) and Lyman H. Andrews (b. ~1806, Rensselaer Co., NY / VT) ended up in Marshall County, Indiana, and both come from the Vermont–New York border region. The circumstantial case for a family relationship is real but not documented. Complicating factors:

  • Perviz reported his birthplace as Vermont; Lyman's was recorded as both Rensselaer County, NY (Old Settlers) and Vermont (son's death certificate)
  • A "Lyman Andrews" married Livvy Tinny in Pownal, Bennington Co., VT, in July 1838 — but this may be a different Lyman Andrews. Marshall County Lyman's son Charles was born in March 1838 in Wyoming County, NY, to Mary Jane Yeakley, not Livvy Tinny. The timing and geography create a disconnect.
  • No shared household on any known census, no probate record, no family bible, and no document explicitly names them as relatives
Unproven — Julia's Maiden Name and Origins

Julia is consistently identified as "Julia C. Andrews" in Indiana records. "Odell" is the family-reported maiden name, but no marriage record for Perviz Andrews and Julia Odell has been located. Possibilities:

  • Odell was her true maiden name, with "C" as a middle initial
  • Odell was a prior married name, and "C" represents her actual maiden surname
  • The Perviz baptism in Washington County, NY (1843), places him between Troy and Bennington — the right geography for him to have met Julia. But her specific origin and parentage remain unknown.
Unproven — Perviz's Parents

Several Andrews families lived in Bennington County, Vermont, in the 1790s–1810s:

  • Isaac Andrews and John Andrews — Shaftsbury, 1800 census
  • Ralph Andrews — Arlington, 1800 census
  • Asa Andrews — Arlington, 1830 census. A Cynthia L. Andrews (b. ~1799, Arlington) named Asa Andrews as her father and Elizabeth as her mother when she married in 1859. If Cynthia is Perviz's sister, then Asa and Elizabeth are the parents. This is not proven.

No document yet found names Perviz's father or mother.

Unproven — Perviz on the 1850 Census

Perviz Andrews should appear on the 1850 Marshall County census with Julia and children George W. and Cynthia Ann. He has not been located. He may be listed under a mangled spelling of his first name (Purvis, Pervis, or even just P. Andrews), or he may be in an adjacent county or township not yet searched.

Confirmed — White Family Connection

Per family knowledge: Cynthia E. Andrews married Orrin White, brother of Hortense White who married Cynthia's brother George W. Andrews. Double sibling marriage. The White family came from Michigan. Cynthia and Orrin lived in Northern California; Nellie visited by train from San Francisco.

Next Steps

Priority 1 — Find Perviz on the 1850 Census

Search Marshall County and St. Joseph County, Indiana, 1850 Federal Census for any Andrews household headed by a male born ~1802 in Vermont, with wife born ~1818 in New York. Try spellings: Parvis, Pervis, Purvis, P. Andrews. Also search adjacent counties (Fulton, Starke, Elkhart). If found, the household will confirm Julia's birthplace, the children's ages, and Perviz's occupation and neighbors.

Priority 2 — Search the Marshall County Democrat

The Plymouth newspaper is digitized from November 15, 1855, onward at Hoosier State Chronicles (newspapers.library.in.gov). Perviz died before July 1856. A death notice in this paper could give age, cause of death, and origin — details that would be unavailable elsewhere for this era. Search for "Andrews" in all 1856 issues.

Priority 2B — Find Cynthia E. Andrews/LaFong Death Record

Cynthia E. Andrews married four times, last known as Mrs. William H. LaFong, residing in Walkerton, St. Joseph County, Indiana, as of November 1920. Her death record — search St. Joseph, Marshall, Noble, and LaPorte counties for a Cynthia LaFong dying after 1920 — would name her parents. This is potentially the fastest route to identifying Perviz's father and mother, since Indiana death certificates from this era typically include parents' names. Note: the surname of her third husband is believed to be Adle, not Able.

Priority 3 — Washington County, NY Marriage Records

Perviz was in Washington County in July 1843 (Camden Valley baptism). Cynthia Ann was born in Indiana in January 1844. If Perviz married Julia in Washington County, the marriage should fall between ~1841 and mid-1843. Search Washington County marriage records for Andrews–Odell or Andrews–[C surname] in that window.

Priority 4 — Camden Valley Church Records

The Camden Valley Church of the United Brethren records (Washington County, NY) contained the baptism. The full membership roll, marriage register, and other records may contain more Andrews entries, or Odell/O'Dell entries. Contact the Washington County Historian or check for additional digitized records on the county's DocumentCenter.

Priority 5 — Trace the Harris Family

Amos Harris and family members were baptized alongside Perviz in 1843 in Washington County. Stephen Andrews married Lucretia Harris in Marshall County in 1863 (officiated by Austin Fuller). If the Harris families in both locations are the same, this documents a group migration from Washington County to Indiana and connects the Andrews families. Search for Harris households in both locations.

Priority 6 — 1810/1820 Census, Bennington County & Rensselaer County

Search for Andrews households in Bennington, Pownal, Shaftsbury, and Arlington (Bennington County, VT) and Hoosick, Petersburg, and White Creek (Rensselaer County, NY) in the 1810 and 1820 censuses. Look for a household with boys in the age brackets matching Perviz (b. 1802) and potentially Lyman (b. ~1806). These censuses record only tick marks, not names, but the right household composition could identify the father.

Priority 7 — Resolve the Lyman Andrews Identity

Determine whether the Lyman Andrews who married Livvy Tinny in Pownal, VT (July 1838) is the same Lyman H. Andrews of Marshall County, Indiana. Key conflict: son Charles was born March 1838 in Wyoming County, NY, to mother Mary Jane Yeakley — four months before the Pownal marriage. Possible explanations include Mary Jane dying and Lyman remarrying quickly, or two different Lyman Andrews. Search for Mary Jane Yeakley's death in Wyoming County, and check the original Pownal marriage register (not the index card).

Priority 8 — Studebaker Family Genealogy

"The Studebaker Family in America" is a multi-volume published genealogy at the Indiana State Library. Lydia Andrews married Abraham W. Studebaker in 1850, with Daniel Andrews officiating. This book likely discusses the Andrews connection and may contain information about which Andrews family Lydia belonged to.

Priority 9 — 1908 Marshall County History, Volume 2

"A Twentieth Century History of Marshall County" by Daniel McDonald (1908) contains biographical sketches in Volume 2. A "B. M. Andrews [b. 1904]" appears in the biographical sketches collection at FamilySearch, with genealogical information tracing the father and grandfather. This could connect to the Andrews families in this research. Both volumes are free online at Internet Archive and FamilySearch Digital Library.

Assessment: Are They Related?

The database search found no confirmed family relationship between Lyman and Perviz in the documentary record. However, the database search also surfaced new geographic data that refines the picture:

Revised Geography

Lyman had land in Genesee County, NY (western New York, near Buffalo) in the 1830s before moving to Indiana. Perviz was in Chautauqua County, NY (also western New York, south of Buffalo) in 1840. These counties are about 60 miles apart — both in western New York, not the eastern VT/NY border area. This is a different geographic cluster than previously assumed and worth investigating.

However, Perviz was born in Bennington, VT (eastern), was baptized in Washington County, NY (eastern) in 1843, and appeared in the 1850 census in Salem, Washington County, NY (eastern). His trajectory may have included a stint in western NY (1840) before returning east, then finally moving to Indiana.

Timing Problem — Perviz Fulmer

The 1860 census lists Perviz Fulmer as age 5, placing his birth at ~1855. But Julia didn't marry Jonas Fulmer until July 10, 1856 — after Perviz Andrews died in April 1856. If the boy was truly born in 1855, he was born before Julia married Jonas, and while Perviz Andrews was still alive. Possible explanations: (1) the age is slightly off and he was born in late 1856 or early 1857, after the marriage; (2) Julia and Jonas's relationship began before Perviz's death; (3) the child is actually Perviz Andrews's biological son, adopted by Jonas. The name "Perviz" for the child makes most sense as a memorial to the deceased first husband.

Wisconsin Land Patent — 1848

Investigation of a federal land patent issued to Pervis Andrews in Dodge County, Wisconsin. March 28, 2026.

The Patent

Confirmed — BLM / General Land Office

Pervis Andrews and Edward West (joint patentees) received 142.58 acres in Dodge County, Wisconsin, on May 10, 1848. Cash purchase through the Green Bay Land Office at $1.25/acre (~$178 total, split between two buyers). Located in Township 11N, Range 16E, Section 18 — present-day Burnett or Westford Township, south-central Dodge County. The parcels were "government lots" along a water feature.

What Was This About?

Perviz didn't move to Wisconsin. He was a clothier in Salem, New York, in 1850 — two years after this purchase. Neither he nor his partner Edward West appears in any Wisconsin census. This was almost certainly speculative land investment, not homesteading.

The late 1840s were the peak of Wisconsin's first land boom. After the Black Hawk War cleared Native title and federal surveys opened millions of acres, land agents traveled to eastern cities — setting up in taverns, courthouses, and churches to sell Wisconsin land by the parcel. They'd show plat maps, describe the water features and mill sites, and take cash on the spot. Groups from the same community would buy lots in the same section on the same day.

That's exactly what the record shows. Three patents were issued on May 10, 1848 for land in the same section: Perviz Andrews and Edward West, plus James R. Whaley. A land agent almost certainly came through the Washington County, New York area in early 1848, and these men bought in together. Perviz and West split the cost of a premium waterfront parcel. The plan was probably to flip the land or relocate later. When neither happened, the land was likely sold or abandoned. Perviz moved to Indiana instead, around 1853–1855.

Other Andrews in Dodge County

A Lucas Andrews (also spelled "Lucus") patented land in the adjacent township (12N-15E) in 1846 and 1848. The proximity was noted but explored — no connection to Perviz was established. The name does not appear in Washington County, NY or Marshall County, IN records alongside the known Andrews families.

Other Names to Trace

  • Edward West — Perviz's joint patentee. A business partner, neighbor, or possibly relative by marriage. Search Washington County, NY census and land records for an Edward West connected to the Andrews or O'Dell families.
  • James R. Whaley — filed on the same date in the same section. Likely part of the same batch purchase. May have been from the same New York community.

What Happened to the Land

The Dodge County Register of Deeds and the BLM Tract Book (Volume 958) would show whether Perviz sold the land, when, and to whom. If the land was still in his name when he died in 1856, it would have passed through his estate — and any probate record in either New York or Indiana could reference it.
